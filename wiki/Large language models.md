A large language model — an entity trained on large samples of text to answer natural-language queries on any topic in complete grammatically correct paragraphs.

## There's immense power in predicting the next word in a sentence 

What are LLMs really?

> Just word math.

[On with Kara Swisher: Joanna Stern](https://podcastindex.org/podcast/5665900?episode=54869807814), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=2bd59a60a0596bf67566fc606970267b).

It turns out natural language processing was always less about implementing the rules of grammatically correct sentence structure and more about predicting the next word in a sentence from statistical distributions in large samples of text.
