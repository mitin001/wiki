Circadian disruption — the result of exposure to light at night and a risk factor for breast cancer in women and prostate cancer in men. 

## Mitigation

If light at night cannot be avoided, circadian disruption can be mitigated. When scheduling someone for night shifts, do not schedule them for more than three nights in a row.

> Experts concluded that night shift work schedules that reduce circadian disruption may reduce breast cancer risk. They recommended that night shift schedules have no more than three consecutive night shifts.

[NTP Review of Shift Work at Night, Light at Night, and Circadian Disruption](https://ntp.niehs.nih.gov/research/assessments/cancer/completed/shiftwork).
