## Policy proposal: the least taxing tax 

Scott Galloway explains that when we decide who pays what in taxes, we should think about how much loss in happiness that would cause. If the tax is so high relative to one's income that the person has to ration basic necessities, that's too taxing. This is why we have tax brackets: wealthy people don't have to ration basic necessities if taxed at a rate at which poor people would.

> If you start taxing education and food and housing, you end up with homelessness, less educated people, people who put off their mammogram and die of metastatic breast cancer. Those are taxes that are too taxing. An alternative minimum tax on corporations making above a billion dollars, and people, say, making over 3 million a year, they don't lose any happiness. And also do away with the estate tax exemption. If the Bezos children inherit 20 billion instead of 30—no loss in happiness. But that 10 billion that could potentially be divided amongst the million, $10,000 to the million poorest households—a lot of incremental happiness.
